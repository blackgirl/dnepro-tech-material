    Lecithin is widely used in the food industry as a food additive (E-322). Lecithin is a surface active agent – it works well at the phase boundary of different substances. At presence of two immiscible liquid phases, lecithin lowers the surface tension and acts as an emulsifier. When interaction between the solid and liquid phase is necessary, lecithin acts as a wetting and dispersing agent. When using between the solid phases, the substance acts as a lubricating agent. Lecithin is also gave a good account of itself as an agent helping to release products from forms (reduced adhesion to forms).

    While the production of bakery products the use of lecithin leads to better dough processibility, better volume, and increasing keeping time.

    Lecithin stops moisturizing and minimizing wafers crumbling giving them a pleasant color texture and natural crunch.

    At producing crackers, cookies, muffins and pies lecithin application improves the shortening properties and acts as an agent to release products from the mold. Lecithin may also be used in the food industry as an antioxidant (a substance that prevents oxidation).

    The use of lecithin makes it possible to obtain a homogeneous finely-porous crumb compact structure with a flat frame. Sunflower lecithin is an emulsifier which allows you to adjust the acidity and strengthen the gluten frame.

    Lecithin is highly soluble in the dough and allows you to adjust its consistency. Emulsifying properties of the powder simplify the dough machine processing by giving it the necessary dryness. The result of the lecithin usage is a significant decrease in the percentage of defective products.

    In addition, lecithin can replace eggs, if their use is provided for preparing by the recipe. Lecithin will not only become a full-fledged alternative to eggs, but also improve the performance of the dough such as layering and structure.

    The use of lecithin in confectionery provides the dough with strength, its color with uniformity, texture and structure with smoothness. Another lecithin property is to give the finished product a uniform color and reduce the risk of cracking.

    At production biscuits, thanks to lecithin, elastic dough is easily formed. Cookies, prepared with the help of lecithin, become pleasantly crispy, so there is an opportunity to reduce the fat content. Sunflower lecithin reduces the need in fat by 20 – 25% in cookies or any other products. Moreover, lecithin slows fat oxidation, whereby cookies remain fresh longer.

    Sunflower lecithin also helps to form a thin layer around the yeast particles in frozen pizza, thereby protecting the product from shock freezing and damage.

    Suggested dosages of lecithin: from 0.5% to 2% from flour mass.
